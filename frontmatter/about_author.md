# About the Authors

## Dr Payton Yau
*(also known as Tung On Yau)* Dr Yau is a Lecturer in Computational Biology and Biological Sciences at **Nottingham Trent University** (NTU), Nottingham, United Kingdom, and an Associate Faculty member in the Department of Health Science at the **University of the People**.

He completed his MSc in Biomedical Science and his PhD by Publications — both at Nottingham Trent University — building a research programme that sits at the intersection of biotechnology, computer science, and biomedical data analysis. His laboratory work spans both wet lab and dry lab environments, and his research has been published across fields including microbiology, molecular biology, bioinformatics, gastroenterology and hepatology, computational biology, genomics, and public health.

His published work includes studies on colorectal cancer biomarkers and faecal screening methods, transcriptomic analysis of inflammatory bowel disease, metagenomic characterisation of antimicrobial resistance, cortisol and cytokine profiles in depression, and the epigenetic regulation of oncogenes in gastrointestinal cancer. This breadth reflects a consistent methodological commitment: wherever there is biological data, rigorous quantitative analysis is required to interpret it correctly.

It is that commitment — and the recognition that health science students rarely receive adequate preparation for it — that produced this book.

## Dr Suhirthakumar Puvanendran
*(also known as Dr S. Puvanendran or Dr Suhirth)*

Dr Puvanendran is a Bioinformatics Researcher and Computational Scientist with a PhD specialising in Multi-Omics Integration and Machine Learning. He currently supervises and examines MSc Bioinformatics research projects at **Birkbeck, University of London**.

He completed his MSc in Bioinformatics with Distinction and his PhD in Bioinformatics — both at the **University of West London** — where he developed a computational framework for early disease prediction using transcriptomic and variant signatures integrated with AI-based classification models. He is also a **Registered Nurse (Adult)** holding a BSc (Hons) in Nursing and Healthcare, a combination that gives him a translational perspective spanning over a decade of clinical practice and cutting-edge computational research.

His research centres on the early detection of lung adenocarcinoma through multi-omics and machine learning approaches, including a PROSPERO-registered systematic review and meta-analysis (CRD420261303156) and the development of deep learning classifiers validated across independent gene expression cohorts. His published work includes a cell-free RNA gene expression study identifying gestational week 13 as a critical window in pre-eclampsia pathogenesis.

It is his dual identity — as both a clinician who has worked at the bedside and a computational scientist who builds machine learning pipelines on HPC clusters — that informs his conviction that quantitative literacy is not optional for health science graduates. It is clinical infrastructure.
